Penguins

Penguins are a species of aquatic, flightless birds. Despite species varying in size and shape, most have a distincitve black and white patterning. This is actually a form of camoflauge; when predators swimming beneath the penguin look up, their white stomachs blend with the light, and when predators above them look down, their black backs blend in with the dark deeper ocean.

There are 18 species of penguins in total. Of these species, 17 of them can only be found in the southern hemisphere. The only species that crosses over the equator is the little blue penguin, also known as the fairy penguin.
